Loans: an interactive data visualisation

A project for the Udacity Data Analyst Nanodegree

Introduction

When we think about loans, we often think about the economic and societal problems surrounding it. We think about interest rates, the general probability of defaulting or bankruptcy. We think about the economic implications for countries, but not so much about the actual people getting these loans, and the mundane things they get their loans for: home improvement, a motorcycle, or maybe even a surgery. The following is an attempt to show the people behind the loans: the jobs that they have and the reasons for getting a loan. It is an interactive visualisation using loan data set from Prosper.

I used R to do some data wrangling on the original data set. I only used data from after 2009 for two reasons: there has been a change in documenting the credit rating in the data set, and secondly I believe that the world has changed a lot during the crisis of 2008/2009, especially when it comes to the financial decisions that people make. I wanted to use data that reflects the way people think about money nowadays. Next, I started with a concept drawing of the data visualisation I was thinking of. I first wanted to make a scatter plot with dots representing all the individuals so you could clearly see that these are all unique people with different jobs, different types of loans, and different ratings. I quickly realised that although the idea was interesting, plotting 80.000 dots was not going to make a lot of sense.
